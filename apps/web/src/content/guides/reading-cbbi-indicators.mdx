## Why read indicators individually?

The composite CBBI score is useful for a quick read on cycle position, but individual indicators tell more nuanced stories. Two cycles can both reach CBBI 85 while their constituent indicators disagree — one driven by MVRV and NUPL signalling genuine euphoria, another reaching the same composite score with Pi Cycle and Reserve Risk still lagging.

Understanding the individual signals lets you assess *why* the composite is where it is.

<Admonition type="note" title="Prerequisite">
  This guide assumes familiarity with what the composite CBBI score represents. If you're new to CBBI, read [Understanding the CBBI Score](/guides/understanding-cbbi-score) first.
</Admonition>

## Indicator overview

The nine indicators split into three categories by what layer of market behavior they measure:

**Price/market structure**
- Pi Cycle Top
- 2-Year MA Multiplier
- Bitcoin Trolololo (log regression)

**On-chain supply economics**
- MVRV Z-Score
- RHODL Ratio
- Reserve Risk
- Puell Multiple

**Sentiment / network activity**
- NUPL
- Woobull NVT

## Reading Pi Cycle Top

Pi Cycle fires a binary signal: either the 111d MA has crossed above 2× the 350d MA, or it hasn't. In CBBI, this binary is smoothed into a continuous 0–100 score based on the *distance* between the two moving averages.

```
score = normalize(distance_to_crossover, historical_range)
```

When Pi Cycle's sub-score is above 80 but the crossover hasn't fired yet, the two MAs are converging rapidly — the signal is imminent. This is more actionable than waiting for the binary trigger.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the indicator breakdown table">
    On the CBBI dashboard, expand the indicator panel below the main chart. Each row shows the current sub-score, a sparkline of historical values, and the raw metric value.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compare Pi Cycle to NUPL">
    Pi Cycle is a price structure signal; NUPL is a sentiment signal. When both are above 70 simultaneously, the signals are corroborating. When only one is elevated, look at why.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the confidence band">
    Wide confidence band at high composite = indicators disagree. Narrow band = they agree. High composite + wide band is less actionable than high composite + narrow band.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## MVRV Z-Score in depth

MVRV Z-Score is one of the highest-signal indicators for identifying overvaluation:

```
MVRV = Market Cap / Realized Cap
Z-Score = (Market Cap − Realized Cap) / std_dev(Market Cap, all time)
```

The key thresholds historically:

| Z-Score | Interpretation | CBBI sub-score |
|-|-|-|
| < 0 | Coins underwater on average | 0–15 |
| 0–3 | Normal range | 15–55 |
| 3–6 | Elevated, approaching historical tops | 55–80 |
| > 6 | Extreme overvaluation (past tops only) | 80–100 |

<Admonition type="warning" title="Z-Score alone is insufficient">
  MVRV Z-Score can spike temporarily during a sharp price rally without a sustained top. MVRV needs corroboration from sentiment signals (NUPL) and market structure (Pi Cycle) before treating it as a definitive top signal.
</Admonition>

## NUPL sentiment zones

Net Unrealized Profit/Loss maps directly to named behavioral zones that correspond to identifiable market phases:

```typescript
function nupl(marketCap: number, realizedCap: number): number {
  return (marketCap - realizedCap) / marketCap
}
```

| NUPL | Zone | What it means |
|-|-|-|
| < 0 | Capitulation | Average holder underwater |
| 0–0.25 | Hope/Fear | Recovery from capitulation |
| 0.25–0.5 | Optimism | Majority in profit, holding |
| 0.5–0.75 | Belief | Accumulation of unrealized gains |
| > 0.75 | Euphoria | Near-universal profit, peak greed |

The transition from Belief (0.5–0.75) to Euphoria (> 0.75) is historically the highest-signal zone transition for identifying late-cycle risk.

## Reserve Risk: the HODLer conviction metric

Reserve Risk is different from all other CBBI constituents in that it explicitly models long-term holder behavior:

```
Reserve Risk = Price / HODL Bank
HODL Bank = cumulative daily Bitcoin days destroyed (BDD) forgone by holders
```

When price is high but HODLers aren't selling (BDD remains low), opportunity cost is high but conviction is higher. Reserve Risk spikes when HODLers finally distribute into the rally — historically a late-cycle signal.

<Admonition type="tip" title="Reserve Risk divergences">
  In 2021, Reserve Risk spiked in April then fell back while price continued higher through November. This divergence (metric cooling while composite remained elevated) foreshadowed the split-top structure of the 2021 cycle, where HODLers distributed early and then re-accumulated before the final move.
</Admonition>

## Practical: building a personal dashboard read

When opening the CBBI dashboard, develop a consistent read sequence:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check the composite and confidence band">
    Get the overall position and the confidence level. Wide band? The next steps matter more.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Separate price from sentiment">
    Pi Cycle + 2Y MA + Trolololo are price structure. NUPL + Reserve Risk are sentiment. Are they agreeing?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the supply-side">
    MVRV + RHODL + Puell. Miner behavior (Puell) diverging from holder behavior (MVRV) at high scores is notable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check NVT last">
    Woobull NVT is the network activity signal. If all other signals are elevated but NVT is low, transaction activity doesn't support the valuation — typical of speculative pumps.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Conclusion

Individual indicator reading turns CBBI from a single number into a structured argument. Two cycles at the same composite score can have completely different risk profiles depending on which indicators are driving it.
